About Me

I probably need a brief introduction for internet users in recent years. Some see me as an unstable adventurer who disrupts certainties, while others see me as offering a refreshing alternative viewpoint on various topics. Over time, I realized that I don’t mind what people think!

For the benefit of those who have stumbled onto this website by “accident”, I’ll give you a summary about me.

My name is Chavez Poon, and my baptismal name is “Cyrillus Mariae”. I was born in Hong Kong on the 14th of June 2003. In my family, some members are Catholic such as my father, also my aunt is a nun of the Sisters of the Precious Blood, her name is Sister Bernadette M. Y. Yeung. But sadly, she passed away in 2020.

My childish imagination is divided between historical Church buildings, Traditional liturgies, and the Sacred arts. Under the influence of my primary school teachers. I was baptized as a Roman Catholic in April 2012 inside my primary school chapel by a Priest of the Prelature of Opus Dei.

I am an experienced Acolyte and Master of Ceremonies, and I have served in different parishes and communities in the past 10 years. Now I usually serve the Holy Mass and various liturgies as Master of Ceremonies by invitations and appointments. My situation has remained unchanged since then.

For some reason, I started exploring the Latin liturgical rites in the Catholic Church, and now I’m interested in the Tridentine Rite, Novus Ordo, Milanese Ambrosian Rite, Spanish Mozarabic Rite, and the French Lyonese Rite.

In my daily life as a Roman Catholic, of course, not every day is perfect. Looking back at my life, I realize God has already uniquely prepared the path for me and guided me every step of the way. I give thanks to God for so many wonderful people who support me, love me, comfort me, encourage me, and pray for me. And also I give thanks to God for all that I have and all that I am, especially for my more than 10 years as a Roman Catholic. I feel so blessed, happy, and contented.
