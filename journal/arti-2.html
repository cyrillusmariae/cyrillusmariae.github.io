Fixing some "serious" tech issues

Yesterday, my Mum gave me a second-hand desktop computer from her office, which is a Lenovo ThinkCentre. Originally, that desktop had been wiped clean, so I reinstalled Windows 10 on it. However, after installing the operating system, I encountered some "serious" tech issues, including the inability to connect to the internet and poor graphics.

To address these problems, I attempted to install the drivers from the internet using my MacBook Air, which runs Windows 10. I saved the drivers on a USB drive and plugged it into my ThinkCentre desktop. Unfortunately, after the installation, the Wi-Fi selection didn't display any networks. Fortunately, I was able to connect the desktop to the internet using an Ethernet cable, so I plugged it in, and the desktop successfully connected.

Next, I downloaded and installed the graphic drivers from Lenovo's website. As a result, the screen's resolution returned to normal, and the graphics became clear.

I must say, encountering such issues is quite common when acquiring a second-hand computer.
